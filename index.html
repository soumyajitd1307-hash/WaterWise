Conservation Guide

Water Saving Tips

Simple everyday habits that can save thousands of litres a year — for your household and the planet.

💡 Did You Know?

The average person wastes over 30 litres of water every single day through leaky taps, long showers, and inefficient appliances. Small changes in daily habits can collectively save millions of litres across communities.

⏱️
Shorten Your Shower
Cutting your shower from 10 minutes to 5 minutes saves 40 litres per person every single day. Use a waterproof timer or a playlist to stay on track.
Saves ~14,600 L/year
🚿
Install a Low-Flow Showerhead
Standard showerheads use 10-12 L/min. Low-flow models use just 5-7 L/min with no noticeable difference in pressure. One-time change, lifelong savings.
Saves ~18,000 L/year
🚽
Use a Dual-Flush Toilet
Old single-flush toilets use 9-12 L per flush. Modern dual-flush systems use just 3-6 L. If you can't replace it, place a filled bottle in the tank to reduce volume.
Saves ~10,000 L/year
🔧
Fix Dripping Taps Immediately
A tap dripping once per second wastes over 11,000 litres a year. Check all taps monthly and replace worn washers — it costs less than ₹50 and takes 10 minutes.
Saves ~11,000 L/year
💚
Hydrate Smart — Drink Tap Water
Skip bottled water and drink filtered tap water instead. Bottled water requires 3-4 litres of water to produce every 1 litre sold. Keep a reusable bottle filled in the fridge.
Saves ~2,000 L/year & ₹5,000+
👕
Only Run Full Laundry Loads
A washing machine uses the same amount of water whether it's half-full or completely full. Wait for a full load before running a cycle to maximise efficiency.
Saves ~8,000 L/year
🍽️
Soak Dishes, Don't Rinse Under Running Tap
Running the tap while rinsing dishes wastes up to 20 L per session. Fill one basin with soapy water and another for rinsing — equally effective and far more efficient.
Saves ~7,000 L/year
🌿
Water Your Garden at Dusk or Dawn
Watering during midday causes up to 50% evaporation before the water even reaches roots. Evening or early morning watering keeps moisture in the soil far longer.
Saves ~5,000 L/year
🪣
Collect Rainwater for the Garden
A simple rooftop collection barrel can harvest hundreds of litres during a single monsoon shower. Use it freely for plants, mopping, and washing vehicles.
Saves ~20,000 L/year
💧
Reuse Cold Warm-Up Water
Keep a bucket in the shower and collect the cold water while you wait for it to heat up. This clean water is perfect for flushing toilets, watering plants, or mopping floors.
Saves ~3,000 L/year
🧼
Turn Off the Tap While Brushing
Leaving the tap running while brushing teeth wastes 6 litres per minute. Turn it off while brushing and only turn it on to rinse — a habit that takes one day to form.
Saves ~4,000 L/year
🚗
Use a Bucket to Wash Your Vehicle
A hosepipe uses 150-200 litres to wash a car. Two buckets of water achieve the same result using only 15-20 litres. That's a 90% reduction per wash.
Saves ~5,000 L/year
🥗
Shift Towards a Plant-Rich Diet
Producing 1 kg of beef requires approximately 15,400 litres of water. Replacing even one meat-based meal per week with a plant-based one dramatically reduces your water footprint.
Saves ~50,000 L/year
